BUS 310: Business Analytics Fundamentals

Spring 2026 | Tuesday & Thursday 2:00‚Äì3:15 PM | Sawyer 421

Instructor: Dr. Jordan Mitchell Email: jmitchell@suffolk.edu Office: Sawyer 829 Hours: Tue/Thu 3:30‚Äì4:30 PM

Course Description

This course introduces students to the foundational tools and techniques of business analytics. Students will learn to collect, clean, analyze, and visualize data to support business decision-making. The course emphasizes hands-on experience with industry-standard tools including Excel, Python, and AI-powered analytics platforms. By the end of the course, students will be able to transform raw data into actionable business insights.

Learning Objectives

  1. Apply statistical methods to analyze business datasets and identify meaningful patterns
  2. Use AI tools (including ChatGPT and Claude) to accelerate data analysis workflows
  3. Create professional data visualizations that communicate analytical findings
  4. Build predictive models using regression and classification techniques
  5. Document analytical processes for reproducibility and validation

Assignments & Assessments

Assignment 1: Data Cleaning Challenge Individual | 10% | Due: Week 3

You will receive a messy dataset from a retail company containing sales transactions, customer information, and inventory records. Your task is to clean and prepare this data for analysis using Python. Document all cleaning decisions and transformations applied.

Deliverable: Cleaned dataset (.csv) + Jupyter notebook documenting all cleaning steps

Assignment 2: Exploratory Data Analysis Individual | 15% | Due: Week 5

Using the provided healthcare operations dataset (patient wait times, treatment outcomes, resource utilization), conduct a comprehensive exploratory data analysis. Identify key patterns, outliers, and relationships in the data. You must use at least one AI tool to assist your analysis and document your prompts.

Deliverable: Jupyter notebook with analysis, visualizations, and AI prompt log

Assignment 3: Predictive Modeling Project Team of 3 | 20% | Due: Week 9

Your team will build a predictive model for a bank's loan approval system. Using historical loan data (applicant demographics, credit history, income, employment), develop a classification model that predicts whether a loan applicant will default. Your goal is to maximize prediction accuracy while minimizing false negatives (missed defaults).

Deliverable: Technical report including model methodology, performance metrics (accuracy, precision, recall, AUC), and Python code

Assignment 4: Dashboard Development Individual | 15% | Due: Week 11

Create an interactive dashboard using the provided supply chain dataset. The dashboard should enable users to explore supplier performance metrics, delivery times, defect rates, and cost trends. Include appropriate filters and drill-down capabilities.

Deliverable: Functioning dashboard (Tableau or Power BI) + 1-page technical documentation

Assignment 5: AI-Augmented Case Analysis Team of 3 | 20% | Due: Week 14

Your team will analyze the "DataDriven Retail" Harvard Business case. Use AI tools strategically to extract insights from the case's supplementary dataset (50,000 customer transactions). Compare the efficiency and depth of AI-assisted analysis versus traditional manual approaches. Document which analytical tasks benefited most from AI augmentation.

Deliverable: Case analysis report (max 10 pages) + complete AI prompt documentation showing iterative refinement

Final Examination Individual | 20% | Week 15

Comprehensive examination covering analytical methods, tool applications, and interpretation of results. Format includes multiple choice, short answer, and a mini-case requiring data interpretation and recommendation.

Course Policies

Attendance: Regular attendance is expected. More than three unexcused absences will result in a grade reduction.

Late Work: Assignments submitted late will receive a 10% penalty per day. No submissions accepted more than three days past the deadline.

AI Use Policy: AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, etc.) are permitted and encouraged for all assignments in this course. All AI-assisted work must include complete documentation of prompts used. Learning to work effectively with AI tools is a core skill this course develops.

Academic Integrity: All submitted work must represent your own effort and understanding. While AI tools may assist your process, you must be able to explain and defend all analytical choices.
